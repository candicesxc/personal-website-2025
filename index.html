About

I first learned to code in college, playing with small web experiments that turned passing ideas into something I could interact with. I wasn’t chasing technical perfection I just loved the quiet thrill of making something appear from nothing. That early curiosity never really left.

After graduation, I found myself drawn into marketing, fascinated by the mix of insight, creativity, and data that shapes how people understand products and stories. I worked across teams from fast moving startups to Fortune 50 companies and learned how ideas evolve when they meet real users.

Now at Yale for my MBA, I know I’m not meant for a future in banking or accounting, even though I can handle more spreadsheets than I ever expected. Instead, I circled back to code through vibe coding. I start with a feeling and translate it into a structure, an interaction, or a tool that gives an idea form.

Below are a few of those creations each one a moment when code and AI helped me explore a question, test a spark, or build something that felt meaningful to make.

How I Work

Start with a feeling

Every project starts with a vibe: a frustration, a pattern I notice, or a question I cannot shake. I map that feeling into simple user journeys and decide what a minimum delightful version could look like.

Prototype in the browser

I use AI assisted coding tools to move quickly from idea to clickable prototype. I care about readability, clean flows, and narratives that make complex concepts feel obvious once you see them.

Ship, learn, and remix

I launch early, gather feedback, and then remix what works into the next experiment. Over time, these small projects add up to a library of patterns, prompts, and stories I can reuse.
